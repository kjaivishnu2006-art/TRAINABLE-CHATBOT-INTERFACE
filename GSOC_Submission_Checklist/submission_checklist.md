# GSoC Submission Checklist

This checklist will guide you through the process of applying for Google Summer of Code.

## Phase 1: Preparation (Pre-Application)
- [ ] **Explore Organizations:** Review the list of accepted mentoring organizations (once announced).
- [ ] **Select 2-3 Favorites:** Narrow down organizations that match your skills and interests.
- [ ] **Study Project Ideas:** Read the project ideas page for each organization.
- [ ] **Join Communication Channels:** Join their Slack, Discord, or Mailing Lists.
- [ ] **Introduce Yourself:** Make a polite introduction in the community channels.
- [ ] **Start Contributing:** Look for "good first issues" or "help wanted" tags to make your first small contributions.

## Phase 2: Proposal Drafting
- [ ] **Review Proposal Guidelines:** Check if the organization has its own proposal template or requirements.
- [ ] **Draft Your Proposal:** Use the provided template and tailor it to the project.
- [ ] **Get Feedback Early:** Share your draft with potential mentors and iterate based on their comments.
- [ ] **Finalize Timeline:** Ensure your weekly milestones are realistic.
- [ ] **Convert to PDF:** Save your final proposal in PDF format for submission.

## Phase 3: Submission
- [ ] **Register on GSoC Portal:** Complete your profile on the official GSoC website.
- [ ] **Upload Proposal:** Submit your proposal(s) before the deadline (March 31, 2026, 18:00 UTC).
- [ ] **Verify Eligibility Documents:** Be ready to provide any requested proof of eligibility (ID, school enrollment, etc.).

## Phase 4: Selection & Beyond
- [ ] **Stay Active:** Continue contributing to the project even after submission.
- [ ] **Check Results:** Monitor the GSoC portal on announcement day (April 30, 2026).
- [ ] **Community Bonding:** If selected, participate actively in the bonding period.
